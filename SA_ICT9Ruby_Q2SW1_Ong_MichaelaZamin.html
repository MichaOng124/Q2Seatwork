10 Different Cat Breeds

Photo Description
Sphinx Cat
The Sphinx cat is one of the only cat breeds in the world where the cats have no fur. Instead, they are wrinkly and bare skinned.
Scottish Fold Cat
The ears of this cat fold in on themselves, hence the name of the breed. This is caused by the cartilage in them being more flexible than the ones in our human ears.
Chausie Cat
The markings of this cat are similar to that of a leopard's. They actually are the distant cousins and descendants of leopards, thus they have a very energetic personality during play.
Maine Coon Cat
These cats are named after the US state Maine where they originate. They have a very thick coat of fur which makes them more suitable for cold climates.
Burmese Cat
Burmese cats are energetic when young, and are very skilled at jumping and climbing. They have a tendency to be territorial around other cats, so it's best if they were your only cat.
Calico Cat
They are most known for the three colors of their coat- white, orange, and black. White is the main color while orange and black are dotted randomly across.
Ragdoll Cat
They come in colors of beige, brown, cream, grey, and most notably, tortoiseshell (named after the color scheme of a tortoise shells'). They also have distinct blue eyes which are large and round.
Siamese Cat
These cats have black paws reminiscent of black gloves. They are very playful, but also mischievous as they attack any small object they find.
British Shorthair Cat
These cats have a distict blue coat earning them the nickname 'British Blue'. They heavily shed their short hair, so they require a lot of grooming.
Munchkin Cat
The cute name of these cats from the fact that they have short, stubby legs. Nonetheless, they are still as energetic and playful as any other cat.
